Abstract

Effective image captioning relies on both visual understanding and contextual relevance. In this paper, we present two approaches, BFC-Capb-a novel background-based image captioning and its extension BFC-Capf-frequency-guided, to achieve the above goals. First, we develop an Object-Background Attention (OBA) module to capture the interaction and relationship between objects and background features. Then, we incorporate feature fusion with spatial shift operation, enabling alignment with neighbors and avoiding potential redundancy. This framework is extended to transform grid features into frequency domain and filter out low-frequency components to enhance fine details. Our approaches are evaluated using traditional and recent metrics on MS COCO image captioning benchmark. Experimental results show the effectiveness of our proposed approaches, achieving better quantitative scores as compared to the relevant existing methods. Furthermore, our methods show improved qualitative captions with more background and concise contextual information, including more accurate information regarding the objects and their attributes.


Below we list the key contributions of our work:

  • We present two image captioning frameworks, BFC-Capb and its extended variant BFC-Capf , with multiple visual features, including background and high-frequency features, in addition to object-level region features and their interactions.

  • We propose a background feature extraction module that pays dedicated attention to the background by removing grids that overlap with the bounding boxes of the object-level region features, and an OBA module to capture the interactions and relationships between the object features and background features.

  • We propose a high-frequency grid features extraction module to enhance fine details that are critical for thorough visual understanding and to enrich the overall context in captions.

  • We introduce MSCA that reduces the modality gap between vision and language, and captures the interaction of text features with high-frequency grid features, objects, background, and their relationships.

  • Extensive experiments on MS COCO image captioning benchmarks with both traditional and recent evaluation metrics confirm the effectiveness of our approaches in generating captions with more background and concise contextual information in terms of both quantitative and qualitative measurements.
